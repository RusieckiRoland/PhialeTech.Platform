# ADR-002: Implementing the Decorator Pattern for Enhanced Control Functionality

## Status
Applied

## Context
In the development of interactions between auxiliary classes like `ViewportManager` and a physical WPF control, there is a requirement for a design pattern that enables extending control functionalities without direct modifications. This includes the augmentation of the control with capabilities such as printer support and the management of default graphic attributes.

## Decision
The decision has been made to utilize the Decorator pattern by introducing the `DeviceDecorator` class. This class will employ the Decorator pattern to enhance the WPF control's functionality, allowing it to handle printer-related operations and store default graphic properties independently from the control. This approach prioritizes simplicity and quick implementation, opting for readiness to adapt to immediate changes over preparing for unknown future complexities.

## Consequences
- **Positive**:
  - Ensures simplicity in design and ease of implementation.
  - Facilitates a quick response to immediate requirements without over-engineering for future scenarios.
  - Promotes code readability and maintainability by keeping the control and its extended functionalities separate.
- **Negative**:
  - May lead to challenges if the system's complexity increases significantly, as the Decorator pattern can become cumbersome with multiple layers of functionalities.
  - This approach might limit the system's scalability in the long term, potentially necessitating a future redesign.

## Alternatives Considered
- The Facade pattern was considered for its ability to provide a simplified interface to complex systems but was not chosen due to its limitations in dynamic functionality extension.
- The Mediator pattern was also evaluated for managing complex interactions but was set aside in favor of a more straightforward approach.

## Future Consideration
If the system evolves to include more elements or requires handling more intricate interactions, this decision may need to be revisited. The Facade or Mediator patterns could be reconsidered to better manage increased system complexity and maintainability.

## Notes
- This solution prioritizes simplicity and rapid implementation, focusing on the current requirements and maintaining agility for changes, over attempting to devise a solution for unknown future complexities.
